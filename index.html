Clickbait Challenge

The Task

Develop a classifier for the detection of clickbait in social media streams. More on clickbait.

For each social media post in the dataset, the post on the social media platform itself as well as the linked target web page are provided as JSON-Object. Example: 

              
{"post_id": "608999590243741697",
 "post_timestamp": "Thu Jun 11 14:09:51 +0000 2015",
 "post_text": "Some people are such food snobs <target_link>",
 "post_media": ["608999590243741697.png"],

              

"target_title": "Some people are such food snobs",
"target_paragraphs": [
  "What a drag it is, eating kale that isn't ...",
  "A new study, published this Wednesday by ...", 
  ...]}

Classifiers have to output a clickbait score in the range [0,1], where a value of 1 denotes extreme clickbait. 


{"post_id": "608999590243741697", "clickbait_score": 0.7}

For determining the winner, we use ROC-AUC as primary evaluation measure. For informational purposes, we compute further evaluation measures such as PR-AUC and the runtime of the software.

How To Participate

  1. Register for the challenge to get a TIRA virtual machine.
  2. Develop and train a clickbait classifier on the training data.
  3. Deploy the trained classifier on a TIRA virtual machine.
  4. Use TIRA to evaluate the trained classifier on the test set.
  5. Submit a notebook paper and present your approach at the workshop.

Software Submission

We ask you to prepare your software so that it can be executed with two arguments for input and output locations via a command line call.

 > mySoftware -i path/to/input/directory -o path/to/output/directory

You can choose freely among the available programming languages and among the operating systems Microsoft Windows and Ubuntu. We will ask you to deploy your software onto a virtual machine that will be made accessible to you after registration. You will be able to reach the virtual machine via ssh and via remote desktop. More information about how to access the virtual machines can be found in the user guide below:

Virtual Machine User Guide »

Once deployed in your virtual machine, we ask you to access TIRA at www.tira.io, where you can self-evaluate your software on the test data.

Note: By submitting your software you retain full copyrights. You agree to grant us usage rights only for the purpose of the Clickbait Challenge. We agree not to share your software with a third party or use it for other purposes than the Clickbait Challenge.

Paper Submission

Paper submissions consist of up to four (4) pages of content. Final versions will be given one additional page so that reviewers’ comments can be taken into account.
